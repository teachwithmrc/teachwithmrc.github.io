Research-Based Scenarios

5 more examples built around actual teacher workflows

These versions lean harder into the left-library + right-generate idea. The goal is not just “more layouts,” but to test which structure wins in different teaching scenarios: quick whole-group prep, intervention groups, search-heavy use, batch planning, and repeat-use favorites.

Progressive disclosure Research consistently supports revealing complexity in steps instead of dumping every option at once. That makes accordions useful, but only when they support a clear active path.
Recognition over recall Recent, favorite, and saved sets reduce mental effort because teachers recognize options they already trust instead of rebuilding choices every time.
Stable navigation rails Desktop productivity tools work well with persistent left rails when the rail holds reusable navigation or filters and the main pane shows immediate results.
